the nature of order

Tiny Stroke Changes the Whole

To better understand the idea that life comes from the relationship between the Whole and individual “centers” within the Whole, see Christopher Alexander’s extensive essay The Nature of Order.

The whole contains all possibilities but by itself is static.

The smallest mark changes the whole, defining a center of attention.

The dot creates a set of possible vectors, allowing dynamism and vitality that wasn't visible before.

A simple dot on a blank page creates the possibility for volumes and spatial areas that were not visible before.

The vectors and spatial areas defined by the dot create new "centers" or areas of interest and potential.

The blank sheet of paper represents wholeness. An unbroken, undivided whole.

It starts with a blank page
